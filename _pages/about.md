## Social Psychopharmacology Lab

We study how psychoactive drugs shape human social behavior — and how to use that knowledge to treat psychiatric illness.

Social dysfunction is among the most debilitating features of conditions like schizophrenia, depression, and PTSD. It predicts poor outcomes, reduces quality of life, and shortens lifespan. Yet no pharmacological treatments specifically target social symptoms. **Our lab works to change that.**

We conduct controlled human behavioral studies using MDMA, serotonergic psychedelics, opioids, and other compounds to probe the neuropharmacology of social motivation, affective processing, and interpersonal bonding. We combine behavioral assays, neuroimaging, and psychophysiology to understand mechanisms — and to translate findings into clinical interventions.

The lab is directed by **[Dr. Anya K. Bershad](/anyabershad-com/cv/)**, Assistant Professor-In-Residence in the Department of Psychiatry & Biobehavioral Sciences at UCLA, and recipient of the NIH Director's Early Independence Award.

---

#### Research Focus

- **MDMA & prosocial behavior** — leveraging MDMA's empathogenic effects to treat social deficits in schizophrenia and PTSD
- **Opioids & social motivation** — investigating buprenorphine as a modulator of social reward in schizophrenia
- **Psychedelics & affective processing** — controlled studies of LSD microdosing and serotonergic psychedelics on mood and cognition
- **Social stress pharmacology** — how drugs modulate responses to psychosocial stress
